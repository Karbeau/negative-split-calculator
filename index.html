What is a Negative Split?

And why would I want one?

A negative split is a racing strategy that involves completing the second half of a race faster than the first half. It is intentionally setting a slower initial pace, followed by a gradual or sudden increase of speed towards the end

Alternate strategies include even splitting (racing at a steady pace) or sit and kick (also known as a sprint finish). Conversely, the act of completing the first half of a race faster than the second half is known as a positive split.
